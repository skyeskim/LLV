Village Toddler Program · Ages 1–3

The world is brand new.
Let them discover it.

Montessori and Waldorf inspired. Project-based. Child-led with gentle guided rotations. Bilingual from day one. Farm-to-table snacks prepared in-house. A program that meets your toddler exactly where they are — and walks with them as they grow.

🏺

Montessori Inspired

Child-led choice, practical life skills, sensorial learning, and real independence built through beautiful, intentional work.

🌻

Waldorf Inspired

Natural materials, storytelling, seasonal rhythm, music, and imagination — because toddlers learn through their whole body.

🔭

Project-Based

Real questions, real exploration, real pride. When children are invested in a project, they are unstoppable.

🤱

The Coziness of Auntie

Your child will experience the warmth of familiar faces, tummy time challenges, and the joy of seeing the world for the first time.

🥦

Farm-to-Table Snacks

Healthy snacks prepared in-house daily. Children help wash, pour, and prepare. Real food, real learning, real nourishment.

🇲🇽

Bilingual Daily

English and Spanish woven naturally through every activity, every story, every song. Language is a gift we give early.

How It Changes By Age

Same village. Same love.
Different depth every year.

A 13-month-old and a 35-month-old live in completely different developmental worlds. Our curriculum shifts meaningfully at each stage.

1

The Explorer — Ages 12–24 Months

Everything is new. Everything is fascinating. Our 1-year-old curriculum is built around safe, rich sensory exploration, the foundational language explosion, and the warmth of a village that knows them by name.

Sensorial & Motor

Treasure Baskets & Sensory Play

Natural objects — wood, fabric, metal — to touch, explore, fill, and dump. Fine motor development through joyful discovery.

Heuristic playMontessori inspired
Language & Literacy

Word Flooding & Books

We name everything, all day. Board books in English and Spanish, songs, and simple two-word phrase modeling.

Bilingual dailyDaily read-alouds
Practical Life

I Can Do It Myself

Pouring, wiping, washing hands — real Montessori practical life work gives toddlers competence and pride from the earliest age.

Montessori inspired
Art & Expression

Process Art — Not Product

Natural paints, sensory dough, finger painting — exploring freely. The process is the curriculum at this age.

Waldorf inspired
🌙

At age 1, we follow their lead completely. Attention spans are short — that is perfect. We offer rich environments and warm, responsive caregivers who celebrate every single discovery.

2

The Builder — Ages 24–36 Months

Two-year-olds are intense, passionate, and brilliant. They have opinions (loudly), they have ideas, and they are discovering who they are. Our curriculum channels that power into building, creating, and connecting.

Projects

Our First Big Projects

What do seeds need? Where does water go? Multi-day explorations with real investigation and real outcomes.

Project-basedScience & wonder
Language

Storytelling & Story Acting

Children dictate narratives, act out stories, and begin to sequence events. Language explodes and we fuel every flame.

Bilingual storiesPuppet play
Social & Emotional

Learning to Be With Others

Sharing, naming feelings, using words — two is the year of big emotions and our curriculum meets them with compassion.

SEL: Feelings vocabulary
Practical Life

Real Responsibility

Setting snack tables, watering plants, classroom jobs — two-year-olds love real work. We give them real roles and real recognition.

Farm-to-table snack prep
⚡

Two is not terrible. Two is extraordinary. The passion, the intensity — that is a powerful mind waking up to itself. We honor it, guide it, and celebrate every outburst of personhood.

3

The Leader — Age 3

Three-year-olds are ready for bigger questions, longer projects, real friendships, and the beginning of academic foundations. Our curriculum deepens everything — and at age 3, a conversation with your liaison can open the door to our Farm School program.

Early Literacy

Letters, Sounds & Stories

Letter recognition, phonemic awareness, and the beginning of pre-reading — woven into play, never worksheets.

Montessori sandpaper letters
Early Numeracy

Numbers in the Real World

Counting snacks, measuring ingredients, sorting — math is everywhere and three-year-olds are ready to find it.

Cooking measurementsMontessori beads
Deep Projects

Long-Form Inquiry

Two and three-week projects on nature, community, animals, seasons. Three-year-olds can sustain deep interest.

Documentation panelsChild-led questions

Farm School · Ages 4–7

Where the classroom
meets the earth.

A 35-week, full-spectrum school program rooted in farm-to-table living, STEAM discovery, bilingual immersion, and the belief that children learn best when they are outside, moving, building, and growing something real.

🌾

Farm-to-Table

Children plant, tend, harvest, and cook real food. Science, nutrition, and life skills in one beautiful daily lesson.

🔬

STEAM Learning

Science, technology, engineering, art, and math — always hands-on, always real-world, always connected to the land around them.

📚

35-Week Curriculum

Fully sequenced from Foundations to Graduation. Literacy, numeracy, social studies, SEL, arts — all differentiated for PreK and Kindergarten.

🇲🇽

Bilingual Daily

Spanish and English woven into every week. Vocabulary, stories, songs, and conversation — two languages, one village.

💛

SEL Every Week

Social-emotional learning is threaded through every unit. Feelings, cooperation, conflict resolution, and community responsibility — always.

🎓

Week 35 Graduation

Portfolio presentation, memory book, and a ceremony that celebrates a full year of becoming. Families gather. Good tears are guaranteed.

✨

Is Your 3-Year-Old Ready?

It is possible to get your 3-year-old into our Farm School program — if the child is showing readiness. This is a personal conversation, not a form. Speak directly with Skye Parapat, your liaison, and she will assess your child's readiness together with you.
