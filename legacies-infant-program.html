Developmental Milestones

What your baby is
capable of — at every age.

Our curriculum is built around the natural developmental arc of your infant's first year. Tap each age range to see what milestones we celebrate and how we support them — every single day.

💛

Every child is beautifully different. These milestones are general developmental guides in partnership with Dr. Ogwara, M.D. — not a checklist. We meet your baby exactly where they are. See full disclaimer below.

Social & Emotional
Recognizes Your Voice

Baby begins to calm at familiar voices and shows early social smiling around 6–8 weeks.

What We Do Consistent caregivers, gentle talking, singing, and responsive holding so your baby always hears a voice they are learning to love.
Motor Development
Tummy Time & Head Lift

Building neck and shoulder strength through supervised tummy time — foundational for all future movement.

What We Do Daily supervised tummy time sessions on clean floor mats with a caregiver at eye level, encouraging and talking to baby throughout.
Language & Literacy
Early Sound Recognition

Baby begins distinguishing speech sounds and responds to tone and cadence — the first building blocks of language.

What We Do Reading aloud daily — books like Mommy, Five Little Ducklings, and bilingual board books in English and Spanish. Your baby is never too young for stories.
Sensory
Visual Tracking & Focus

Baby begins tracking moving objects and responding to high-contrast patterns and faces.

What We Do High-contrast visual materials, gentle mobiles, face-to-face interaction, and natural light to support healthy visual development.
🌙

0–3 months is about safety, warmth, and rhythm. We keep our youngest babies in the calmest environment — small group, consistent faces, gentle routines. Your newborn will be held, talked to, and sung to. Always.

Social & Emotional
Laughing & Cooing

Baby begins laughing out loud, cooing, and engaging in back-and-forth "conversations" with caregivers.

What We Do Peek-a-boo, responsive talking, singing back and forth, and making space for baby to "answer" — this is their first conversation and we take it seriously.
Motor Development
Rolling & Reaching

Baby begins rolling front to back, reaching for objects, and bringing hands to mouth — major motor leaps.

What We Do Safe floor play with age-appropriate toys to reach for, supervised rolling practice, and plenty of space to explore movement freely.
Language & Literacy
Babbling Begins

Baby starts producing consonant sounds (ba, ma, da) and responds to their name being called.

What We Do Daily reading of Children's Illustrated stories, Mommy, and bilingual books. We name everything around baby and narrate our actions throughout the day.
Sensory & Cognitive
Cause & Effect Play

Baby discovers that shaking a rattle makes sound — a foundational cognitive leap toward understanding the world.

What We Do Sensory baskets, rattles, soft instruments, and textured objects — always with a caregiver narrating what's happening so language and exploration grow together.
🌤️

3–6 months is when your baby's personality begins to shine. We celebrate every laugh, every coo, every new reach. Our caregivers keep detailed daily notes so you never miss a moment — even when you're at work.

Social & Emotional
Stranger Awareness

Baby begins showing preference for familiar caregivers — a healthy sign of healthy attachment development.

What We Do Consistent assigned caregivers so your baby always has known, trusted faces. We never rotate strangers through — your baby's attachment security matters deeply to us.
Motor Development
Sitting & Crawling Prep

Baby begins sitting with support, moving toward independent sitting, and may begin early crawling movements.

What We Do Floor time with cushioned support, crawling encouragement with motivating toys just out of reach, and celebration of every inch of movement.
Language & Literacy
Understanding "No" & Names

Baby begins to understand their name and simple words like "no" — receptive language is blooming.

What We Do Reading Treasures for Three Year Olds and bilingual stories aloud. We speak to babies in full sentences, narrate everything, and introduce Spanish words naturally throughout the day.
Feeding & Independence
Introduction to Solids

Many babies begin solid foods around 6 months — a major developmental and nutritional milestone.

What We Do We follow your pediatrician's guidance (including Dr. Ogwara's recommendations) and your family's preferences exactly. Farm-to-table whole food philosophy informs everything we offer.
🌿

6–9 months is a season of big leaps and big feelings. Your baby is becoming a little person with preferences, humor, and opinions. We are honored to witness it — and we document everything so you see it too.

Social & Emotional
Waving, Clapping & Play

Baby waves bye-bye, claps hands, and engages in interactive social games — emotional connection is fully blooming.

What We Do Circle time with songs, clapping games, peek-a-boo variations, and intentional social play with other infants in a safe, small group setting.
Motor Development
Pulling Up & First Steps

Baby may begin pulling to stand, cruising along furniture, and taking first independent steps — the world is about to get much bigger.

What We Do Safe furniture to pull up on, wide open floor space, encouraging hands ready to catch and cheer. We will cry happy tears with you when those first steps happen.
Language & Literacy
First Words

Baby may say their first real words — "mama," "dada," "no," "more" — and understands far more than they can say.

What We Do Rich language environment all day. Bilingual reading, naming games, singing, and responding to every sound baby makes as though it is the most important thing ever said. Because it is.
Cognitive
Object Permanence

Baby understands that things exist even when out of sight — a critical cognitive leap. Hide and seek suddenly makes perfect sense to them.

What We Do Hide-and-find games, peek-a-boo, nesting toys, and simple puzzles that challenge and delight your baby's growing mind.
☀️

9–12 months is pure magic. Your baby is almost a toddler — and every day brings something new. Our village is here for every first word, every first step, every belly laugh. We are as proud as you are.

💛

Every Child is Beautifully, Perfectly Different.

The developmental milestones shared on this page are general guides based on widely accepted pediatric research and informed by our partnership with Dr. Fred Ogwara, M.D. of Springs Pediatrics. They represent typical ranges — not fixed expectations.

Some babies roll at 3 months. Some at 5. Some say their first word at 9 months. Some at 14. This does not mean anything is wrong. It means your child is on their own timeline — and that timeline deserves to be honored, not rushed.

This is precisely why we partner with pediatricians rather than rely on generic programs. Your baby's curriculum at Legacies is developed with your child's specific development, your family's values, and your pediatrician's guidance in mind. We do not apply a one-size-fits-all approach to the most important developmental window of your child's life.

If you have concerns about your baby's development, we will always encourage you to speak with your pediatrician — and we are honored to be a resource alongside that care.
